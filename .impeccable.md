## Design Context

### Users
This is a personal blog written by a solo author. Readers come here for programming notes, reading reflections, and life observations. The interface should support calm long-form reading and lightweight discovery of recent posts, topics, and archives.

### Brand Personality
Clean, restrained, fresh. The emotional goal is to feel thoughtful and warm without becoming decorative or sentimental. The blog should read like a clear desk with a notebook on it: orderly, personal, and alive.

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction is minimal, rational, and warm. Support both light mode and dark mode. Use `luolei.org` as a reference for article listing structure: simple category navigation, clear post chronology, and list-first browsing rather than card-heavy marketing layouts. Use `impeccable.style` as a reference for execution quality: strong spacing discipline, intentional typography, and avoidance of generic AI-looking UI patterns.

### Design Principles
1. Prioritize reading over decoration. Content hierarchy must stay obvious at a glance.
2. Keep layouts spare, but not sterile. Use warmth through type, spacing, and softened neutrals rather than ornament.
3. Prefer list-based information architecture for posts, taxonomy, and archives. Avoid grids of repetitive cards unless the content truly benefits.
4. Support both light and dark themes with equal care. Neither mode should feel like the default with the other tacked on.
5. Reject generic AI blog aesthetics: no purple gradients, no glassmorphism, no card-on-card stacks, and no overbuilt hero sections.
